About T1D & Breakthrough T1D

This tournament's proceeds support Breakthrough T1D (formerly JDRF), the leading global organization funding type 1 diabetes (T1D) research and advocacy.

  • What is T1D? A chronic autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. It is not caused by diet or lifestyle, and there is currently no cure.
  • Who it affects: About 1.5 million people in the U.S. and 9.5 million people worldwide are living with T1D; it can be diagnosed at any age.
  • Daily impact: People with T1D must take insulin every day (by injection or pump) to survive and manage blood sugar.
  • Breakthrough T1D's mission: Funding research toward cures and better treatments, improving access to insulin and technology, and supporting the T1D community.
  • Learn more: breakthrought1d.org/t1d-basics
  • Support this fundraiser: Donate via our team fundraising page

Tournament Rules

Scoring

  • Games are played to 11 points, win by 2 (cap at 15 if needed to finish on time)
  • Traditional side-out scoring — only the serving team can score a point
  • Server starts each service sequence calling the score as: server score, receiver score, server number (e.g. "4-2-1")
  • Standard double-bounce rule applies: each side must let the ball bounce once before volleying
  • No volleying inside the non-volley zone ("the kitchen")
  • Let serves are played, not replayed (ball touching the net on serve and landing in is good)

Round Robin & Advancement

  • Each checked-in team plays every other checked-in team once during pool play
  • Standings are ranked by: 1) match wins, 2) total points scored, 3) head-to-head result
  • Top teams from the round robin advance to the single-elimination bracket
  • The bracket is seeded using final round robin standings, with byes given to the top seeds if the field isn't a power of 2
  • Bracket matches are single elimination — one loss and a team is out

Conduct

  • Be on time for scheduled matches — courts run on a fixed time slot all day
  • Self-officiate fairly; call your own lines and faults honestly
  • Good sportsmanship is expected from all players, all day
